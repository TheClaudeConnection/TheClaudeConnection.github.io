What's included in a standard bathroom renovation

When you compare quotes, it pays to know exactly what you're getting. Click through each stage.

Demolition & preparation

  • Strip out and remove vanity, shower screen, base, bath and tiles
  • Full removal of wall sheeting down to the timber studs
  • Inspection for hidden damage or rot, a critical step many builders skip

Plumbing & electrical

  • Full plumbing and drainage work by licensed trades
  • Electrical work as required, including lighting and exhaust
  • Rough-in coordinated around your new layout

Sheeting & waterproofing

  • New noggins and supports, re-sheeted with water-resistant Villaboard
  • Polyurethane membrane to floor and 100mm up the walls
  • Waterproofing to Australian Standard AS3740, by specialists

Tiling & grouting

  • Wall tiling to 2.1m height with skirting tiles
  • Splashback tiling between vanity and walls
  • Quality tiles from Beaumont and National Tiles, or supply your own

Fixtures & fittings

  • New vanity, tapware, towel rail, mirror and shelving
  • Custom-made framed shower screen, frameless available
  • Supply and install new bath where applicable

Finishing touches

  • Internal painting of walls and ceiling
  • Final professional clean of the bathroom
  • Complete rubbish removal, we leave it ready to use

Bathroom renovation costs in Brisbane vary widely depending on the size, condition and complexity of the project. As a general guide, many standard full renovations sit somewhere in the low-to-high $20,000s, while simpler cosmetic upgrades may cost less and larger or more customised renovations with premium finishes can exceed $40,000.

Because every bathroom is different, we recommend a complimentary consultation and on-site inspection before providing pricing. This lets us properly assess your project and prepare a tailored fixed-price quote, giving you clarity and confidence before any work begins.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a bathroom renovation cost in Brisbane?

It varies with size, condition and complexity. As a general guide, many standard full renovations sit in the low-to-high $20,000s, with simpler upgrades costing less and premium or larger projects exceeding $40,000. Because every bathroom is different, we provide a tailored fixed-price quote after a free on-site inspection.

How long does a bathroom renovation take?

A standard full bathroom renovation usually takes around 3 to 4 weeks, depending on size, complexity and the drying times required to comply with Australian Standards. We can typically start within two weeks of confirming your quote.

Do you provide fixed quotes?

Yes. We provide fixed, transparent quotes so there are no surprises. The main thing that can change a price is water-related damage discovered during strip-out, which we always discuss with you before proceeding.

Do you help with design and selections?

Yes. We help with layout planning and provide floor plans to guide your project. We'll work with you on fixtures and finishes to suit your style and budget.

Can I supply my own fixtures and tiles?

Absolutely, we simply adjust the quote accordingly. If you'd prefer, we can source quality products through our trusted suppliers, including Beaumont and National Tiles for tiling and Highgrove and Reece for fixtures.

Do you handle waterproofing in-house?

Yes. Waterproofing is our specialty and it's carried out in-house, in compliance with Australian Standard AS3740. It's the reason homeowners trust us to renovate as well as repair.

What warranty do you provide?

A standard construction warranty of 12 months on minor defects, 7 years on leaks and structural defects, and 10 years on the waterproofing product we use.

Do I need council approval?

In most cases, standard bathroom renovations don't require council approval. If your project involves major structural changes or significant plumbing alterations, we'll guide you through the process during the consultation stage.

Can you renovate apartment bathrooms?

Yes. We renovate apartment and unit bathrooms and account for access requirements as part of the quote and scheduling.

What happens if asbestos is found?

We strip bathrooms back to the timber studs to check for hidden issues, including asbestos, which is common in older Brisbane homes. If it's found, we arrange safe removal through licensed asbestos professionals and discuss options with you before proceeding. Your safety and compliance always come first.
